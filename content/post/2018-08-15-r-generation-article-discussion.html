R‚Äôs History

  • August 2018 marks the 25th anniversary of R
  • The origin of R is formed by the collaboration of Ross Ihaka (University of Auckland) and Robert Gentleman (University of Waterloo) who met when Gentleman was visited the Auckland campus in 1992
  • They wanted to develop a language as flexible as Scheme but with the added statistical functionality and syntax of the existing S language
  • Over the span of 3 months they developed the first version of what would be the R language - a fun nod to the first letter of both the authors first names
  • It was adopted by the university of Auckland who needed statistical software that worked on Macintosh - talk about being cross platform from the get go!
  • They released it publicly on the S listserv mailing list in August 1993
  • In mid-90‚Äôs they considered turning it into a commercial product, but were luckily swayed away to keep it open access by fellow core member Martin Machler from ETH - phew!üò•.

R‚Äôs Community

  • By mid-1997 the R Core team had 11 members including the original authors
  • In March 1997 the biggest breakthrough occurred - Kurt Hornik and Friedrich Leisch developed CRAN
  • CRAN meant that all libraries and R files were freely available in one place
  • Importantly this open access meant that the original R Core community began to extend to wider data science community via the development of specific libaries!
  • Enter Hadley Wickham, who helped launch some of the most widely used packages including dplyr and ggplot2 (part of his thesis!)
